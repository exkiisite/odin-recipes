Luxurious and easy cream mushroom sauce

This sauce is a very slight modification from a recipe i got from rafmazcooks on Instagram, the exact recipe you can find here.

Prep Cook
15 min 45 min
1

This recipe is inspired by rafmazcooks mushroom eggs recipe, (which you can find here here). Instead of making it a morning egg dish, i decided to skip the eggs and up the ingredient amount and use the sauce for pasta, or any other carbs like spätzle. Aside from scaling up the recipe and omitting the (already optional) cheese, I also like to add leeks and some extra seasonings i pesonally really enjoy which creates a creamy, flavourful cream sauce that has a rich mushroom taste. This recipe takes some time and patience, but trust me its worth it. Also, despite the longer cook time, it all happens in one pan!

Ingredients:

  • 25g of butter
  • One large onion, sliced
  • One large leek, sliced
  • 400g of mushrooms, cleaned and sliced
  • three cloves of garlic, crushed or minced
  • 250ml cream
  • 500ml milk
  • 2tbsp flour
  • Salt, black pepper
  • chili flakes
  • oregano
  • 1 tbsp powedered vegetable broth (a vegetable broth cube im sure works aswell, just no liquid)

Instructions:

  1. First, clean your vegetables thoroughly. Leeks and mushrooms tend to have a lot of dirt on them, so taking them time to properly clean them is definetly nessecary. For the mushrooms, use a stiff brush to brush off all dirt off the surface, this prevents the mushrooms from absorbing any water and taking longer to cook down. However if you do not have a brush, cleaning them in water is fine too, youll just have to cook them abit longer as they absorbed more liquid.
    For the leeks, be sure to rinse them very well under water, especially making sure you get the inbetweens of all the layers, especially towards the root, as that is where most of the dirt is. While youre at it, peel your onion aswell.

  2. Once you prepped all your vegetables, its time to chop them all up. You want to slice everything into thin slices so it cooks evenly.

    3. Note: If you are familiar around a kitchen, you dont have to do one thing at a time. The mushrooms go in first, then the onions and the leeks, if you want to prep your mushrooms and add them to the pan and prep your other veggies while they cook up, feel free. In order to be as beginner friendly as possible i listed everything out step by step.

  3. Add your butter to a large sauce pan over medium - low heat. Once it is melted, add your sliced mushrooms.

  4. You want to cook the mushrooms for a couple of minutes, im not giving a specific timerange because it depends on how much moisture your mushrooms have absobed. Once you add them, they will first sweat out all their water before soaking up all the oil, as you fry them they release the oil back and start crisping up. Once they start releasing the oil again (which means they have significantly shrunk in size), you can proceed to the next step.

  5. Add your sliced onions and leeks at the same time, once you added them, add your salt and pepper to them. Let all of this sizzle for a good 10 minutes to cook them down and allow them to start developing those sweet notes. You'll know theyre done when when they start turning soft and slightly browned, you want to cook them past the translucent stage.

  6. Once your alliums have finished cooking, add your seasonings. How much of each you add ill leave up to you, as i usually do it by feeling. The only thing you should be careful is the powdered vegetable broth, as if you add too much it can become overpowering. So start slow, you can still season more later.

  7. Now it is time for the flour. Add your two tablespoons of flour and immedaitely start mixing everything together. The flour will mix with the juices and seasonings in the pan and cook down into the vegetables, everything will turn try and almost paste like - this is what we want, its going to thicken the sauce in to a creamy rich experience. Cook the flour for about 2-3 minutes.

  8. After you cooked down your flour its time to add your liquid, you can dump it all in at once. Once you do, start mixing it slowly to mix everything together nicely. Let this simmer for 10-20 minutes until thick and creamy.

  9. Once its done, taste for any final seasonings you would like to add. And then serve over pasta or any other carbs you like! You can even go rafmazcooks route and add some eggs to eat over toast, it is basically the same recipe.
