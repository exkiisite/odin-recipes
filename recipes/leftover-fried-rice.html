My quick and easy Fried Rice!

Quick, nutrishious and clean. A fifteen minute meal that whips up in no time!

Prep Cook
5 min 10 min

Fried rice is my holy grail recipe when i have leftover rice. It is quick, easy, nourishing and it's all done in one pan. All you need is some day old rice (older is fine, but fresh rice has too much moisture to properly get nice and crispy.), some protein - im going to be using frankfurter sausages in this case, and whatever vegetables you have around. The ingredient measurements are very lenient in this, just use whatever you have available.

Ingredients:

  • Day old rice
  • 2-3 Eggs depending on how much rice you have
  • Spring Onion
  • Soy Sauce
  • Garlic Powder
  • Salt
  • Pepper

possible replacements/optional ingredients

These ingredients are nice additions or replacements for any of the main ingredients listed.
  • Optional: Frankfurter Sausage
  • Frankfurter sausage has always been one of my favourite sausages. In this, chopping it into small sizes and frying it alongside the rice is a nice flavor boost!
  • Replacement - Garlic Powder:
    • You can use any other seasonings you like, I really like the forward garlic taste garlic powder gives, so I tend to use a lot of it. You may replace this with any seasonings you like, be creative! Experiment! It's just rice.
  • Replacement - Eggs:
  • I'm sure you can use egg substitutes to make this dish. I don't see why it wouldn't work. However I have no experiencing preparing vegan egg dishes, so its probably best to do some more research. Just wanted to give a heads up.
  • Optional: Vegetables
  • You can use any vegetables in here as you like. What i like to do is take a pack of frozen mixed veggies and cook them with my rice when i make it the previous day. This way when i make my fried rice, theres already some vegatables in there. Alternatively you could also just add any fast cooking vegetable you can think of. Peas, edamame, etc.

Instructions:

Disclaimer:

I am going to write out the ingredients in the order I personally do them. If you like to instead prep out your ingredients beforehand, heres a quick to do list before you get to cooking:

  1. Chop up and mix ins and vegetables into small pieces
  2. Crack your eggs into a bowl and mix them
  3. Add a small amount of oil to the pan.
  4. Add your rice, let only the rice fry for a tiny bit to dry it out some more.
  5. Crack your eggs into a bowl and whisk them through with a fork.
  6. After heating up the rice in the pan and breaking up any clumps, add your whipped eggs
  7. With constant motion, fry your eggs and rice. Try to really mix the egg into the rice while also trying to get as much surface area as possible in order to avoid the rice getting mushy.
  8. Start a rythm of flattening out the rice for the most surface area and letting it cook, and inbetween mixing it through before spreading it out again. This way you cook the eggs evenly. I think. Idk i always do it like that
  9. Add some, not all, of your green onion
