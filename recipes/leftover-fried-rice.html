My quick and easy Fried Rice!

Quick, nutrishious and clean. A fifteen minute meal that whips up in no time!

Prep Cook
5 min 10 min
1

Fried rice is my holy grail recipe when i have leftover rice. It is quick, easy, nourishing and it's all done in one pan. All you need is some day old rice (older is fine, but fresh rice has too much moisture to properly get nice and crispy.), whatever vegetables, protein or other mix ins you may have around. The ingredients listed are the bare minimum to make the simplest rendition of this (you could even omit the green onion and just have egg and rice if you wish), but feel free to be creative and add in anything you wish.

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons butter, divided
  • 2 large eggs, whisked
  • salt and black pepper
  • *optional* garlic powder, onion powder and chili flakes
  • 800g cooked and chilled rice
  • 3 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 3-4 tablespoons soy sauce

Instructions:

Note: I recommend first reading through the entire recipe before commencing cooking, knowing whats coming ahead helps you prepare everything and prevents being caught off guard and mistakes.

  1. Prepare your vegetables and eggs, chop your green onions into thin slices all the way to the root, and crack your eggs into a bowl and whisk them until they become an even mixture.

  2. Add your three tablespoons of butter to the pan.

  3. Add your rice to the pan, adding it first allows you to fry the rice a bit and dry it out some more. Let the rice sizzle in the butter for about a minute or two while trying to break up any clumps until it is warmed up.

  4. After heating up the rice in the pan and breaking up any clumps, add your whipped eggs, immediately start mixing the eggs into the rice with a large wooden or plastic spoon, try to keep the rice in constant motion in order to allow the egg to evelop every single grain of rice individually to create a nice, crispy, clump free egg fried rice.

  5. Add your soy sauce, again making sure to mix it in well so it covers everything evenly, be sure to keep the motion up in order to avoid clumps in your rice!

  6. Add your seasonings, follow your heart, but if youre unsure how much to add be sure to go light specifically on the garlic powder, as it can be very overpowering very quickly. Personally i enjoy that, so i like to add alot myself aswell as a good amount of chili flakes for heat.

  7. After seasoning your rice, add in your green onion whites, they have more bite than the greens and adding the whites before the greens allows the whites to cook down abit, lose some of that bite, and create a more varied flavor profile.

  8. After cooking down the whites a bit, add the midde part of the green onion. Leave the very loose greens from the top for a final finishing garnish and a nice freshness to balance out all the complex cooked flavours we developed.

But when do i add my [optional ingredient here]?

You can add in any of your desired mix ins at any point between steps 6 and 7, maybe earlier depending on cook time. If you fancy adding meat or something like bacon that benefits from a nice long render in fat, start with that instead and then add the rice to the meat fat in order for even more flavour!
